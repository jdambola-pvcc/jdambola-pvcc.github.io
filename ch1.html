Computer Science Definitions in Chapter 1

  1. Computer Science: The study of algorithms.
  2. algorithm:A well-ordered collection of unambiguous and effectively computable operations that, when executed, produces a result and halts in a finite amount of time
  3. Sequential operation:Algorithmic structure that carries out a single task and then moves on to the next operation in sequence
  4. Conditional operation:Algorithmic operation that asks a question and selects the next step to carry out based on the answer to that question
  5. iterative operation:Algorithmic operation that repeats a block of instructions
  6. Stored program computer:A model of computation in which the instructions to be executed are represented as binary string and stored in the memory of the computer
  7. Programmer: Someone who uses high-level landuages to solve problems by creating computer programs
  8. Integrated circuit: A discrete electronic components, integrated circuits with transistors, resistors, and capacitor etched onto a piece of silicon

The 5 generations of computer development

  1. 1st Generation 1950-1957: This era saw the appearance of UNIVAC I, the first computer built for sale, and the IBM 701, the first computer built by the company that would soon become a leader in this new field.
  2. 2nd Generation 1957-1965: Heralded a major change in the size and complexity of computers. In the late 1950s, the bulky vacuum tube was replaced by a single transistor only a few millimeters in size, and memory was now constructed using tiny magnetic cores only 1/50th of an inch in diameter.
  3. 3rd Generation 1965-1975: This was the era of the integrated circuit. Rather than using discrete electronic components, integrated circuits with transistors, resistors, and capacitors were photographically etched onto a piece of silicon, which further reduced the size and cost of computers.
  4. 4th Generation 1975-1985: The appearance of the first microcomputer. Integrated circuit technology had advanced to the point that a complete computer system could be contained on a single circuit board that you could hold in your hand.
  5. 5th Generation 1985-present: Where we are today. However, so much is changing so fast that the concept of distinct generations of computer development has outlived its usefulness.

FORTRAN

  1. FORTRAN stands for Formula Translation
  2. FORTRAN was developed in 1957 by John Backus.
  3. FORTRAN was designed as the first standard computer language and dominated the field of computer science for many years
  4. FORTRAN is still in use in many scientific fields such as doppler radar wearther forecasts.

COBOL

  1. COBOL stands for Common Business Oriented Language
  2. COBOL was developed in 1959 to allow programs from different computer manufactures to run with little modifications and to have a language that can be read in ordinary english.
  3. COBOL was used for people in the finance industry and human resources.
  4. COBOL is still used today in many industries and is still in demand
